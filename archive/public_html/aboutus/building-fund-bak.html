We are an active Russian Orthodox Christian community. Our Sunday services are in English with many hymns repeated in Slavonic. We have outreaches to the community in the form of prison ministry, nursing home visitations, and a full liturgical and teaching schedule. We are the only church in the Metroplex which follows the same calendar used in Russia and regularly includes Slavonic and English in our services and homilies.


We have a two phase building plan.

We plan to build our chapel, with a small hall affixed, for services in late 2009. Building started in July 2009, after a very long approval process. The chapel will look like a "real church", with high ceilings, and two or three cupolas, and it will be our worshipping home as we grow and get ready for phase two. It will be built with later expansion to a full, canonical temple in mind. We will later build a church hall, or possibly purchase a house abutting the land, and convert into a church hall. After the new hall is ready, we plan to enlarge the chapel, and use the entire building as a temple. The final design will be in a cross configuration, and be very roomy, with very high ceilings. The chapel will cost about $221,000 to build (about $130 per sq. ft., with a 120,000 loan at 7%, paid off in ten years).
