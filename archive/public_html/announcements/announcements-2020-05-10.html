As previously announced, we have re-opened in a limited way. We can currently allow 20 people to sign up to worship in the church, and 5 in the attached hall with the doors propped open. As part of our ongoing expansion work, the wall separating those spaced is being removed, God willing, this Wednesday. And so starting next week, we can invite 25 people to worship in our newly-expanded nave.

 

If you are able to attend the liturgy on a day other than Sunday, out of consideration to others, please sign up for that liturgy. It may be that we can accommodate everyone in one week, or it might take two weeks. We still must be patient. It is our hope that, as much as possible, whole families will attend together.

 

Remember:

You must observe 6' distancing from everybody who does not live in the same house, and some people will be in the attached hall (with the doors propped open). Parents, please do everything you can to keep your children close.

If you have COVID-like symptoms or are in contact with somebody who does, do not attend.

If you are in an at-risk group, please do not attend.

You are not required to wear a mask but you may if that is your desire.

You should prepare for the Liturgy by attending at least some portion of the evening service on the prior day, unless this is completely impossible. If you wish to commune and cannot attend the previous evening service, you must arrange this in advance with the serving priest.

If you wish to receive Holy Communion, you should confess in advance and prepare as usual.

Catechumens and non-Orthodox family members are welcome and encouraged to sign up and attend.

To sign up: https://signup.com/go/GmQhOMA

We do a Mitey Mites collection every week. There are more challenges now, since the church is closed to the public, but we are still collecting every week for the extreme needs that are becoming more pressing every day. This week, we are collecting for: “Help dad of 3 fight stage 4 cancer”

I want to take a moment and let you know a little about myself.  My name is Kirk.  I have been married to the love of my life for 22 years.  We have 3 children together.  My oldest 2 are 21 and 18.  I am so proud of them.  Both are attending community college.  My oldest Hannah is studying to be a therapist. My son Dean is studying accounting.  My youngest is 10.  She is the sweetest little girl.  She loves to learn, and is constantly asking questions on just about anything you can think of.  Nicholas (Kirk) Broyles
