Christ is risen!   Christos voskrese!  Pra Chris Song  Phun Kun Cheeb Laow!   He’s back!

I’m going to assume that you are the kind of person who likes to hear the good news first. There is a ton of good news.

We are in the midst of expanding the church right now. The North wall has been taken out, and there is a ton of room now! That’s the first piece of good news.  We also have the occupancy permit for the hall, and we have made extensive renovations there that are being finished as we speak.

The second piece of good news is that we are going to increase the number of people that can be at the service. You will be amazed at how big the space appears to be. Basically, what was our church hall now is part of our church. Therefore, we are increasing the number of people that can be at the service during these pandemic times to ***32***, besides readers, singers and servers and cleaners, and our audio-visual department.

The third piece of good news is that I sense a difference in our services from before the pandemic. I think there’s a little more vigor and a little more intensity. That makes my heart very happy. Fr Nicholas and I are also very happy that we cense the church, we are not just mentally censing you, but many of you are standing there in front of us.

There is one piece of bad news. I am canceling the liturgy for the weekday, only this week.

It will take a humongous effort to get the church ready for service on Wednesday, because basically the entire contents of the nave were shoved into the altar. We also need to clean the church floor extremely well, since this is our opportunity, with all the things out of it. If we were organized and had the manpower it would really be really great to paint it also, but will just have to delay that. It is barely possible that we could get it all done tonight, but we would not be able to serve Vespers until very late, and my exhaustion level does not allow for that. It is also not absolutely certain that everything will be finished before Friday. We can definitely count on it being finished by Friday.

We won’t have Vespers and liturgy until Friday and then Saturday. Then on Saturday will have visual and liturgy on Sunday as usual. There are many more spaces now, and you have not even taken advantage of the spaces that were there! Wake up please, my parish!

There are only 13 people presently signed up to come to liturgy on Saturday and 4! to come to liturgy on Sunday.  I’ll be honest with you, that is just not acceptable. Both liturgies will accept 32 people in the nave. After you get this letter, you need to get online and sign up for liturgy.

I feel the need to talk to you about what is happening in our times, and recent episcopal letters that have been sent out. In this age of information and primarily misinformation and TOO MUCH INFORMATION, we get to hear everything from everybody, and part of it is even true.

The only thing that matters in the running of our church and our diocese is what our bishop tells us to do and the guidance of your two pastors. We do the best we can with our limited spiritual abilities, and one of those abilities, thank God, is that we both understand that we cannot have any life if we do not obey our bishop. Archbishop Peter is a prayerful and a kind man, and Fr Nicholas and I are happy to take his guidance, even if there are times when we may not completely agree with it. It doesn’t matter if we always agree with one another, but it does matter that we have peace.

There was an episcopal letter sent out recently - not from our church, but some of you might be aware of it. It says some very rough things. That is the way that that diocese is choosing to order its affairs. You may agree or disagree. You are a human being, and you have a right to agree or disagree with something. As a Christian, you should not feel that you have a right to disparage or to slander. I do not recommend achieving comments in public forums about this letter. You should thank God that we have a bishop who listens very closely to his priests, and his priests love him and respect him. As for the relationship of other bishops and other priests, I cannot know, since I do not know those bishops well. We cannot infer very much from a letter. https://signup.com/go/GmQhOMA

We in the Diocese of Chicago and Middle America will obey our bishop, because where the bishop is there is the church (this is a very famous quote from St. Ignatius, and it has ordered our dogmatic life since he wrote those words, in the second century). We will rejoice that our church is opening more, and if I could wave a magic wand, I would have it that none of you read complaintive and bitter articles about our governments and foreign governments and organizations, and other dioceses, or that you give more opinions online then you pray prayers.

 

When you get down to it, what is important for you today? It is not important whether or not you have a scathing political opinion about this party or that, or this person or that, or this organization or that. It is important that you have prayed today and that you live as a Christian. It is important that you are living with intensity. Intensity means that we are violent – only with ourselves. We are violent against our sins and our passions. It is impossible to be violent against your sins and your passions if you are constantly commenting on other people’s sins and passions.

Read the Gospel today, especially the Gospel of John which we are reading during this Paschal period. Pray morning prayers, and your evening prayers, and especially pray for others. If you haven’t made a list of people to pray for, consider this your 553rd reminder. This is critically important, much more important than whether or not we expose the sins of secular governments, or anyone else.

Please use the sign-up sheet (https://signup.com/go/GmQhOMA), because it has many more positions in it. Remember that there are those who can only go to church on Sunday, and if you can go on Saturday you should consider this your obligation. I cannot obligate you to do this, and God does not even obligate you to do this, but if you look in your heart you might see that your heart obligates you to do this.

If we have a “Full House” Friday, Saturday and Sunday, we will be able to have basically our entire regular parish worshiping together. We will be separated by time, but we will be together in spirit and we will all be in the church, whether it be on Friday and Saturday, or Saturday and Sunday.

Christ is risen! Christos voskrese! Pra Chris Song  Phun Kun Cheeb Laow! He’s back!
