  • Luke 1:28 and "safe spaces"
    • There is the experience of God where there is no fear, and there is the world, which is full of fear. In the aftermath of the presidential election, we can see this contrast clearly. I found out yesterday that high schools were setting up "safe places" where people could cry about the results of the presidential election and give in to their fears. This is a secular response to life. The Christian response is to pray, and read the Gospels, and experience God, where there is no fear. If you are a Christian, do not be faithless and give into secular fears, as taught by the media and people all around us. In the midst of all these things that make people afraid, God is here, if we have eyes to see and ears to hear.
