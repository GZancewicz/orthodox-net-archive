As to miracles, they oftentimes, while they profited another, have injured the one who had the power, by lifting him up to pride and vainglory, or maybe in some other way ...These then let us perform with much dilligence. For if you change from inhumanity to almsgiving, you have stretched forth the hand that was withered. If you withdraw from theaters and go to the church, you have cured the lame foot. If you draw back your eyes from an harlot, and from beauty not your own, you have opened them when they were blind. If instead of satanical songs, you have learned spiritual psalms, being dumb, you have spoken. These are the greatest miracles, these are the wonderful signs.

St.John.Chrysostom. Homily XXIV, 1, on Matthew VII and XXXII, 10, 11, on Matthew IX. B#54, pp. 167, 218, 219
