Gleanings from Orthodox Christian Authors and the Holy Fathers

Deuteronomy 06

'You shall love the Lord your God with all your soul and with all your might' (Deut. 6:5). When I heard the words 'with all your soul' I was astounded, and no longer needed to hear the rest. For 'with all your soul' means with the intelligent, incensive and desiring powers of the soul, because it is of these three powers that the soul is composed. Thus the intellect should think at all times about divine matters, while desire should long constantly and entirely, as the Law says, for God alone and never for anything else; and the incensive power should actively oppose only what obstructs this longing, and nothing else.

St. Peter of Damaskos(24 Discourses; Love)
