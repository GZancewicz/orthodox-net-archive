​The photo above is of our church on the day we celebrated our Heavenly patron, St Nicholas. Our church has been a beautiful place for us to worship in for about 15 years, but it is too small for us now. God has given us many blessings, and now we must find a way to contain them all!

The space that you see needs to be expanded by at least four times for us to contain the people that are trying to come to church on sunday. Sometimes we have trouble containing people who come for Vigil on Saturday! 

We've tried to make some changes, such as having two liturgies, since we are fortunate to have two priests. They start at the same time, one in the hall and one in the temple so that we can all eat together and have our classes afterward. But this is not optimal. And if there is only one liturgy, for instance, when a priest is gone or when we have a great feast, the church is just not big enough for all the people who want to come.

There are other things that we're doing, such as homeschool Co-op, which is now two full days, and we are hoping to expand to a third. We're using our Church Hall, which is a converted house that's nearby, and it's not adequate, but we are making do. 

We are in the strange situation of being "land rich and cash poor". We own everything, but we don't have enough money to build anything new. So we're raising money and trying to increase our income so that we can deal properly with the blessings, that is, the people, God has given to our little country church.

We are trying to raise half a million dollars, which probably should be a million dollars, and we are offering to anyone who donates to our cause with practical gratitude. I personally don't think that just saying thank you is enough. Therefore, we've always prayed for all of our benefactors. This has created a very large list of names, so I have modified our "offer". Anyone who gives any amount can submit a name to be prayed for at every liturgy for the rest of the life of the parish. It's written into our bylaws that these lists of names will be commemorated by every succeeding pastor of the parish. And for every $100 after that, you can submit another name.

Perhaps it seems a little bit mercenary to you, but we had situations where a person would give a dollar and submit a hundred names. And we pray for all those people, but I don't think we can easily take another thousand names very easily.

In the past, I always de-emphasized money, and it was a mistake. I feel an intense responsibility as the rector to provide for this flock, and we cannot minister effectively to the influx of people because they keep coming! I suppose someone might say that this is a good problem to have, and it is, but it is a problem, and we must do something about it. So we are asking you to help us to do something and we will pray for you.

Sometimes, I make a little joke about our little country parish and say, regarding our small size and wealth, juxtaposed to our many activities and "reach": "pay no attention to the man above the curtain", as a cultural reference to the Wizard of Oz. Our little church has a reach beyond only McKinney because of our Internet presence, and mission building (we have one in Quanah, TX). Therefore, I'm appealing to any of you who live far away but have benefited in any way from that which we freely give with great joy to help us, if you can. 

Priest Seraphim Holland, and all the faithful of St Nicholas
