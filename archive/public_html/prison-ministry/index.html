    • The story of the transformation of a self-reliant boy to a God-reliant man. A letter from a prisoner (Adobe PDF format)
      • 2018-06-07
      Also in Format: Word DOC or mp3
      SYNOPSIS:This letter was read to me to me two weeks before this now God-reliant man is to be baptized. I transcribed it from his handwritten copy. I have known him for a year. Prison ministry can be quite exhausting and even very frustrating (the devil is truly in the details), but the rewards are incalculable. It is always amazing and even surprising to see somebody "gets it". I think God gives these kinds of consolations to pastors, and strugglers in general, to keep us going. I have a friend that I tease because she sees metaphor in everything; well, it takes one to know one. I met Pete literally five days before my son +Daniel died last year. Only a few days after the anniversary of his death, will have the privilege of bringing Pete into new life. I have been given permission to propagate this letter. Priest Seraphim Holland

    • We must make extraordinary effort when we are experiencing extraordinary things. About 7 min (mp3 format)
      • E
      2019-08-15
      SYNOPSIS:Reflections from a prison ministry visit. We must make extraordinary efforts when we are experiencing extraordinary things. This applies to prison, and all Christian life, anywhere. People have a tendency to say or even just think: "I need to do what I can to survive". They have a tendency to say "I’m under great stress so I deserve a break". Actually, when were under great stress, we must turn to prayer, and do other extraordinary things. This is one of the secrets to living a transcendent Christian life.
