On Holy Saturday, while cleaning the sanctuary for Pascha, we came across St. John's "Rules for Acolytes" in some things left behind by our beloved Subdeacon Nicholas Carey, who died last August. I believe they were originally printed in Orthodox America some years ago. I share them with the O-List for those who may never have seen them before. David James RULES FOR ACOLYTES Compiled by St. John, Archbishop of San Francisco 1. In order to serve in the altar, it is necessary to have the blessing of the priest, or, in the case of a hierarchal service, the bishop. 2. One must come to church before the beginning of the service. 3. Upon first entering the altar, one should make the Sign of the Cross and three prostrations facing the Altar Table. On Sundays and feastdays of the Lord, the prostrations are replaced by bows from the waist. 4. Only those who are going to serve are permitted into the altar. 5. Ties should be taken off before vesting. 6. After taking off the sticharion, it should be neatly folded or hung in place. 7. In the altar there should be no conversation, no laughing; there should be no running around the church, nor should one eat anything during the services. 8. All acolytes and ordained readers are required to wear sticharions. To take off the sticharion, one should get a blessing from the priest, or, in the case of a hierarchal service, from the bishop. 9. When receiving the Holy Mysteries, acolytes should take off their sticharions, unless they are ordained readers. 10. One must not touch either the Altar Table or the Table of Oblation. 11. One must not walk about the church during the reading of the Gospel or the Epistle, nor between the exclamation "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ..." and the exclamation "And may the mercies of our great God and Saviour Jesus Christ be with you all." 12. Before putting on a sticharion, one should make sure that one's hands are clean. 13. There should be nothing in the altar or the vestry that does not pertain to the services. 14. It is not permitted to sit down while wearing a sticharion. 15. Before going out of the altar with a candle or one of the fans, the acolyte should first cross himself and bow to the icon on the back wall of the altar and then turn and bow to the hierarch or priest standing in front of the Altar Table. On coming back into the altar, this is repeated. 16. One should remember that the altar is the holiest place and one should stand in it with special reverence and close attention to the service. In passing between the High Place and the Altar Table (which should only be done when absolutely necessary), one should make the Sign of the Cross. 17. During the Creed and the Lord's Prayer, all the acolytes should come out of the altar onto the ambo and join in the congregational singing. 18. While the clergy commune, the acolytes should stand reverently in the altar. 19. Before the sermon, the acolytes should come out of the altar onto the ambo in order to listen. 20. Those who violate the "Church Rules for Acolytes" will have to stand on the left cliros until the end of the service.
