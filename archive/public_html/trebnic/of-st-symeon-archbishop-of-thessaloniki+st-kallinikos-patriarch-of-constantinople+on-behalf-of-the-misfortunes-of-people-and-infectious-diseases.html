First Prayer on Behalf of Infectious Diseases to our God in Trinity

By St. Symeon, Archbishop of Thessaloniki

 

Beginningless, eternal, Master God, Father All-Holy, Almighty, with Thy living and good Word, and Thy Comforter Spirit, accept with sympathy the prayers of us sinners.

The only-begotten Son, Who is in the bosom of the Father, Lord Jesus Christ, with Thy compassionate Father and the gladsome Spirit, mercifully accept the entreaties of we who are the least.

O All-Holy and All-Good Divine Spirit of the Father together with the All-Merciful Father and the Son who became man out of love for mankind, accept the distressed voices of Thy humbled people.

The consubstantial and indivisible Trinity, one in reign and nature, the Thrice-Holy Lord, hear the petitions of Thy humble supplicants and through Thy immeasurable goodness forgive our countless offenses.

Through Thy unsurpassable mercy, take pity on us who are justly chastised as Thou art justly enraged.

Through Thy ineffable compassion, save us who are in danger and redeem us from every just threat and anger of Thine.

Expel from us the evil one and all his pursuits and machinations. Divert every sensible and invisible temptation.

Banish every distress and sigh from us, and guard us from famine and pestilence, untimely and unforeseen death and every other misfortune, and take away from us all scandals and tumults that may arise.

And grant us to live in peace and harmony and abide in love with Thee and others, keeping Thy commandments and finishing the course of this present life in a well-pleasing and pious manner through repentance.

Make us worthy also of Thy heavenly and eternal glory and kingdom.

For Thou are our God and in Thee we place our hope, we have no one else but Thee and we place our hope in calling upon Thy name to save us, through the intercessions of the all-holy and ever-virgin Theotokos, Thy philanthropic angels, and all Thy saints.

For Thou art a God of mercy, compassion and love for mankind and to Thee we ascribe glory, to the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit, now and ever and unto the ages of ages. Amen.

Second prayer on behalf of the misfortunes of people and infectious diseases

by St Kallinikos, Patriarch of Constantinople

 

Beginningless King, invisible, unsearchable, incomprehensible, inexpressible, Who encompasses all creation in Thy hand and maintains, permeates and governs it by Thy inexpressible Word!

Who beforehand tolerated the lawlessness of Nineveh. After their repentance and their conversion Thou didst accept them and granted them through Thy exceeding goodness and slowness to anger, despite all their iniquities and trespasses, to accept Thy habitual and unconquerable philanthropy.

Accept also our prayers just as Thou didst with the conversion and repentance of the Ninevites.

Accept our tears and sighs. Accept our humble petitions, for we are not able, due to our sins, to look upon Thee, Who art sinless.

Accept our deep cries, as smoke of judgment, which we send up to Thee, Master.

Accept the supplication of distress of Thy people.

Prevail over the multitude of our sins in the abyss of Thy passion, and grant Thy people redemption, freedom from temptations and forgiveness by cutting off death.

Yeah, Lord our God, hearken unto Thy people at this hour as well as me the sinner, and make the rod of sinners stand upright among the elect righteous, as Thou alone knowest and hast established, and do not judge them.

And may I also, Thy useless servant, glorify Thy All-Holy name, the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit, now and ever and unto the ages of ages, Amen.


Third prayer on behalf of the misfortunes of people

by St Kallinikos, Patriarch of Constantinople

 

All-Good Lord, Creator of all things, Whose mercy is without measure, and love for mankind beyond compare, Who took all our iniquities and was nailed to the cross to sanctify us; taking courage we fall down before Thee in supplication, forbearing Lord, and Thy ineffable and immeasurable philanthropy is as vast as the sea, therefore we cry before Thee, forgiving Lord: Save us.

We do not offer Thee odes and hymns as the Pharisee, but as the publican. Nor as the senseless thief, but like the sensible and thankful one we cry out: Remember me.

Therefore by thine unsearchable mercy prevail over our iniquities, even if we do not suitably repent and convert; and show us the magnitude of Thy philanthropy; as one who is slow to anger show compassion and reconcile Thy people; put a stop to the scythe of death so that it does not advance upon us; make haste to stop the untimely cutting off; put a stop to this destructive pestilential disease which is just; put a stop to the broad sword that is risen against us so that we may not be destroyed; before Thee only have we sinned, and before Thee alone do we fall down.

Yeah, Lord, hear us sinners and Thy worthless servants in this hour and do not repulse our entreaties; overlook our trespasses, voluntary or involuntary, known or unknown, that we may glorify Thine All-Holy Name, the Father, and the Son and the Holy Spirit, now and ever and unto the ages of ages amen.

On Friday 27 March into Saturday 28 March , 2020 (ns) the monks of Mount Athos, in every monastery, skete and cell, held an all-night vigil against the pandemic of the coronavirus. The vigil was dedicated to the Panagia, who is the protectress of the Holy Mountain, as well as the Holy Hieromartyr Haralambos, who is noted especially for his miracles in vanquishing plagues and epidemics, including an epidemic that once infected the monks of the Holy Mountain and which he was responsible for dispelling. In all the monasteries, during Matins, a special canon to the Holy Trinity for deliverance from pestilence was chanted, as well as a canon to the Theotokos and a canon to Saint Haralambos. Also, during the vigil, the following three prayers were read with great emotion that have been specially written for the deliverance from epidemics and pestilence, the first by Saint Symeon, Archbishop of Thessaloniki, and the other two by Saint Kallinikos, Patriarch of Constantinople and disciple of Saint Gregory Palamas.
