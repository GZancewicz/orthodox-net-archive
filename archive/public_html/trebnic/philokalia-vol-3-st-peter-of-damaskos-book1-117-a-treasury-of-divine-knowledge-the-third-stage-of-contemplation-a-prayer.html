St Peter of Damaskos Book1. A Treasury of Divine Knowledge. The Third Stage of Contemplation - A Prayer

St Peter introduces the prayer: "Then, conscious that Thy look is upon me, with all my soul I will cry out and say:"

0 most merciful Lord, I thank Thee, I glorify Thee, I hymn Thee, I venerate Thee, for unworthy though I am, Thou hast found me worthy in this hour to give thanks to Thee and to be mindful of the wonders and blessings - numberless and unfathomable, visible and invisible, known and unknown - that Thy grace has bestowed and still bestows on our souls and bodies.

I confess Thy gifts; I do not hide Thy blessings; I proclaim Thy mercies; I acknowledge Thee, 0 Lord my God, with all my heart, and glorify Thy name for ever. жFor great is Thy mercy towards meЖ (Ps. 85:13), and inexpressible is Thy forbearance and long-suffering over my many sins and iniquities, over the heinous and godless things that I have done, and still do, and will do in the future.

From these Thy grace has saved me, whether they were committed consciously or unconsciously, in word, in action, or in thought. Thou knowest them all, 0 Lord, Searcher of hearts, from my birth until my death; and, abject that I am, I dare to confess them before Thee.

жI have sinned, I have transgressed, I have acted godlesslyЖ (cf. Dan. 9:5), жI have done evil in thy sightЖ (Ps. 50:4), and I am not worthy to gaze upon the height of heaven.

Yet, finding courage in Thy inexpressible compassion, in Thy goodness and tender mercy that excel our understanding, I fall before Thee and entreat Thee, Lord: жHave mercy upon me, 0 Lord, for I am weakЖ (Ps. 6:2), and forgive me my many crimes. Do not allow me to sin again or to stray from Thy straight path, or to injure or offend anyone, but check in me every iniquity, every evil habit, every mindless impulse of soul and body, of anger and desire; and teach me to act according to Thy will.

AMEN

St Peter continues with instruction about praying the Psalter:

After praying in this way you should immediately address your own thoughts and say three times: ж0 come, let us worship and fall down before God our King.Ж Then you should begin the psalms, reciting the Trisagion after each subsection of the Psalter, and enclosing your intellect within the words you are saying. After the Trisagion say жLord, have mercyЖ forty times; and then make a prostration and say once within yourself, жI have sinned, Lord, forgive meЖ. On standing, you should stretch out your arms and say once, жGod, be merciful to me a sinnerЖ. After praying in this way, you should say once more, ж0 come, let us worship...Ж three times, and then another sub-section of the Psalter in the same way.
