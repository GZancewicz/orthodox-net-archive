How can we identify the "Alleluia" days during, for example, the Nativity Fast?


Generally, one can identify the days on which "Alleluia" is appointed by&#32; referring to either the Typicon or the Menaion.&#32; However, since not&#32; everyone has easy access to these books, I have worked out the following&#32; criteria for determining whether "Alleluia" is appointed during one of&#32; the&#32; lesser fast periods:

1.&#32; It must be a weekday, i.e., Monday through Friday.

2.&#32; It must not be during the festal period of one of the great feasts&#32; of&#32; the Lord or the Theotokos.&#32; Such festal periods that fall during the&#32; lesser&#32; fasts are:&#32;

  2. for the Transfiguration (5 to 13 August),&#32;
  5. for the&#32; Entry&#32; of the Theotokos into the Temple (20 to 25 November)
  8. for the&#32; Nativity of Christ (20 to 24 December).
3.&#32; The service in the Menaion must be a simple or double commemoration&#32; with no Apostol or Gospel readings appointed for the Divine Liturgy.&#32;&#32; This&#32; can also be determined from a Liturgical Calendar such as that published&#32; by&#32; the St. John of Kronstadt Press.

During the Apostles Fast, the Typicon and/or Menaion indicate that&#32; "Alleluia" applies to the following days:&#32;

  • 19, 20, 22, 23, 27-31 May,
  • 1,&#32; 3, 5-7, 10, 15, 16, 20, 22 June.&#32;
One might also add the following days,&#32; for which no explicit indication is found in either the Tyicon or&#32; Menaion,&#32; but which meet the above criteria and do not differ appreciably from&#32; other&#32; days on which "Alleluia" is explicitly indicated:&#32;
  • 26 May and 2, 4, 13 June.
During the Dormition Fast, the Typicon and Menaion indicate that&#32; "Alleluia"&#32; applies to the following days:&#32;
  • 3 and 4 August.
During the Nativity Fast, the Typicon and/or Menaion indicate that&#32; "Alleluia" applies to the following days:&#32;
  • 15, 19, 26, 29 November&#32;
  • 1,&#32; 2,&#32; 3, 7, 8, 11, 14, 15, 16, 18, 19 December.&#32;
One might also add&#32;
  • 18&#32; November
&#32;for which no explicit indication is found in either the Typicon or&#32; Menaion,&#32; but which meets the above criteria and does not differ appreciably from other days on which "Alleluia" is explicitly indicated.

It was stated in an earlier posting that one can determine if "Alleluia"&#32; is&#32; appointed by checking the Menaion to see whether or not stichera for the&#32; Theotokos are provided in Vespers for "Lord, I have cried."&#32; However,&#32; this&#32; method is not completely accurate.&#32; For example, when a double&#32; commemoration occurs, three stichera are provided for each saint, but&#32; there&#32; are not stichera provided for the Theotokos.&#32; This occurs on the&#32; following&#32; days for which "Alleluia" is indicated: 6 June, 19 November and 15&#32; December.&#32; Also, on 26 November, no stichera for the Theotokos are&#32; provided&#32; in the Menaion, and the Typicon says that stichera from the Octoechos&#32; should be used.&#32;

It should be noted that, besides the simple service or double service to&#32; which "Alleluia" applies, additional services are sometimes also found&#32; in&#32; the Menaion under a particular date.&#32; Usually, these additional services&#32; are to later saints that are venerated in local churches.&#32; If it is&#32; desired&#32; to perform one of these additional services, then the simple or double&#32; service to which "Alleluia" applies is superceded and the Lenten service&#32; is&#32; not performed.&#32; The simple service is usually then read at Compline.

In general, the performing of Lenten services during the three lesser&#32; fasts&#32; is now somewhat rare.&#32; In many places, the Lenten service is only&#32; performed&#32; on the first day of the Apostles and Nativity Fasts.&#32; Occasionally, I&#32; have&#32; seen the Lenten service performed on other days, but such instances have&#32; been more the exception than the rule.&#32; This is a great pity, because we&#32; have become accustomed to observing these fasts without any Lenten&#32; liturgical expression.&#32; This lamentable state of affairs was apparently&#32; recognized at the Pan-Russian Council of 1917-18, and, according to&#32; Bishop&#32; Afanasij (Sakharov), a prominent expert on litrugics, the Council issued&#32; a&#32; decree "concerning the restoration of the practice of performing&#32; services&#32; with alleluia on the appointed days of the lesser fasts."&#32; However, it&#32; is&#32; difficult to say to what extent, if any, this decree was ever implemented.&#32; If anyone has the full text of this decree available, I would be&#32; interested&#32; in hearing from you.

This discussion comes at an opportune time, since we are about to begin&#32; the&#32; Nativity Fast.&#32; It would be most heartening if churches that conduct&#32; daily&#32; services were now to begin serving the "Alleluia" services on the days&#32; appointed during this Fast.&#32; And for those of us who don't have the&#32; opportunity to attend daily services, perhaps we could add the Prayer of&#32; St. Ephraim, with its prostrations and bows, to our Morning and Evening&#32; prayers on those days.

From Reader Daniel Olson - Posted Nov 26, 1998 NS

The "Alleluia" days in the Nativity fast are the following, as marked in the Typicon, provided they do not fall on a Saturday or Sunday:
&#32;(Dates are Julian):&#32;

  • Nov. 15
  • Nov. 19
  • Nov. 26
  • Nov. 29
  • Dec. 1
  • Dec. 2
  • Dec. 8
  • Dec. 11
  • Dec. 14
  • Dec. 16
  • Dec. 18
  • Dec. 19
Several other days in between those COULD have an Alleluia-service, but there is a service for a later Russian Saint added following the earlier, simpler weekday service for those days.

From Fr John Shaw
Posted Nov 25, 1998 NS&#32;

Alleluia" days are weekdays in the Fasts on which only three stichera&#32; for&#32; the day are appointed to be sung on "Lord, I have cried" at Vespers.&#32;&#32;&#32; More&#32; importantly, and hence the name, is that also "The Lord is God" at&#32; Mattins&#32; is replaced by "Alleluia" with its verses (as in the Great Fast).&#32;&#32; In&#32; the&#32; "lesser" Fasts it is common practice only to observe "Alleluia" days on&#32; Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays, although some (as indicated by one&#32; repsonse to my question concerning the Office of the Typika) suggest&#32; that&#32; they may be observed on all weekdays.&#32;&#32; The fact that only one (or&#32; perhaps&#32; two) such days are indicated for the Fast of the Dormition results on&#32; practice in their not being currently generally observed in that Fast.&#32;
In the longer Fasts of the Apostles and the Nativity rather more days are indicated for this observance.

"Alleluia" days are usually marked as such in the Great Book of Hours.

Those Saturdays on which the service for the Departed is used are also referred to as "Alleluia" days, but the Lenten form of service is not used.

From Archimandrite Kyril Jenner.
