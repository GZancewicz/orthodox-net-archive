  • Slavonic Gospels and Epistle books have several kinds of markings in the margin.
    • One set of markings denotes the prescribes reading for the day, the feast of the Saint.
    • There is another set of markings which are names of weekdays and numbers, which do not refer to the liturgical readings read in church, but are divisions for use in reading the New Testament privately ("in one's kellia").

    This document lists these private reading divisons, up to this time not available in English.

    This system results in the Epistles, minus the Apocalypse which is not printed in the liturgical Epistolary, being read through in 8 weeks, and the Gospels being read through in 4 weeks.

    Since the Revelation (Apocalypse) of St. John is prescribed to be read in some seasons at the Night Vigil, and is in the Slavonic Bible, it is also included in the table below (and adds two additional weeks to the readings).

    This 'Lectionary for the Kellia' can be used in various ways:

    • The Epistles and Gospels are read consecutively.

    • The entire New Testament would thus be read through in 14 weeks, or about 4 times a year.
    • The Epistles, less Revelations, and Gospels are read concurrently.

    • The Epistles would be read through every 8 weeks, and the Gospels twice during the same period (which also coincides with the 8 tones cycle)
    • The Epistles, with Revelations, and Gospels are read concurrently

    • Thus, the Epistles would be read through every 10 weeks, and the Gospels every 4 weeks.

    Thus far the Introductions to each of the Books of the New Testament that are in the Slavonic Epistle and Gospel books have not been published in English.
